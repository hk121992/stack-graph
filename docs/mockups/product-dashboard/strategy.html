Vision · horizon: 2–3 years

Every team reaches its first shared outcome within a day of signing up.

The Product Strategy thesis — the guiding argument every objective, bet, and work item is held against. The vision is the apex above; this is the argument for how we get there.

Diagnosis

New teams stall before their first shared outcome. Value is gated behind configuration they don't yet understand, so they churn before they ever experience the product working.

Guiding policy

Collapse time-to-first-outcome. Opinionated defaults over configurability until the team has a real win — trade flexibility for momentum at exactly the moment momentum decides retention.

Coherent action

A guided first-run that produces a real shared artefact in under ten minutes; defer all admin and configuration until after the first win.

Who & the job

"When my team adopts a new tool, help us produce something real together fast, so we trust it's worth switching." — the design-partner segment first.

Open questions

Does the guided first-run generalise beyond design partners? Will opinionated defaults frustrate power users enough to matter?

Decided · not-doing

Not building deep admin controls pre-activation. Not chasing enterprise SSO this cycle. Not sales-led onboarding (premise killed — see the record).
