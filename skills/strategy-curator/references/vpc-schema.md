# Value Proposition Canvas — schema

The structure for stating a value proposition against one customer segment. Two sides; **fit** is the
claim that the value map addresses the customer profile, and it stays a *hypothesis* until evidenced.

## Customer profile — the segment (observed, not invented)

- **Jobs** — what the customer is trying to get done (functional, social, emotional), with the
  context and the job's importance.
- **Pains** — bad outcomes, risks, and obstacles around the jobs, with severity.
- **Gains** — outcomes and benefits the customer wants (required, expected, desired, or unexpected).

Rank jobs, pains, and gains by importance to the customer — not all carry equal weight.

## Value map — the offer

- **Products & services** — what we offer that the customer engages with.
- **Pain relievers** — how the offer removes or reduces specific *pains*.
- **Gain creators** — how the offer produces specific *gains*.

## Fit

**Problem–solution fit** — the pain relievers and gain creators address the customer's *most
important* pains and gains. State fit as a claim tied to specific profile items and treat it as a
hypothesis to test, not an assertion. **Product–market fit** is fit confirmed by evidence of demand.

## Recording a value proposition

Each entry binds one **value map** to one **customer segment**, lists the profile items it claims to
address, and carries its **evidence state** — `assumed` / `tested` / `confirmed` — with links to the
hypotheses and findings that support or refute it. Unconfirmed items stay explicit; never treat an
assumed item as true.
